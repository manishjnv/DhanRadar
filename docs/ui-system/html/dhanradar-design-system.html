// 02 · Foundations

Brand guidelines.

DhanRadar is the calm, confident voice that turns financial complexity into actionable signal. Wealth, scanned.

Story

Name

DhanRadar
"Dhan" means wealth in Sanskrit and Hindi. "Radar" represents scanning, detecting and signalling. Together: a system that scans the market for wealth-building opportunities you'd otherwise miss.

Tagline

Spot better investments before the crowd.
Promises an edge without overpromising returns. Use the full line in hero contexts; the shorthand "Wealth, scanned." works on small surfaces.

Personality

We are five things and we are not five things.
We areWe are not
Intelligent — we earn trust with depthPretentious — we never talk down
Calm — composed under volatilityHype-y — no rocket emojis, no all-caps
Plain-English — jargon translatedJargon-heavy — "alpha generation" is banned copy
Honest — risk shown alongside rewardSalesy — no fake urgency, no countdown timers
Respectful of beginnersCondescending — "even your dad can use it" is wrong

Voice in microcopy

✓ Yes
  • "DhanRadar Score combines valuation, growth, quality, momentum and risk."
  • "Spot better investments before the crowd."
  • "You're up ₹4,824 today. Mostly Reliance and Bharti."
  • "Markets carry risk. Past returns aren't future returns."
✗ No
  • "🚀 Our AI-powered algo crushes the market 💯"
  • "Beat Wall Street at its own game."
  • "Welcome back, valued investor!"
  • "Guaranteed returns — try free!"

Things to avoid

  • 01Bull/bear cartoons or stock-photo handshakes
  • 02Hindi-only headlines on English surfaces (or vice versa)
  • 03Saturated greens & reds (use our calm tokens)
  • 04Promising returns, percentages or wealth outcomes
  • 05Sans-serifs other than Geist in product surfaces
  • 06Decorative SVG illustrations of buildings, charts, money

Usage rules

Mono is non-negotiable for numbers. Every monetary, percentage and ratio value uses font-family: var(--dr-font-mono) with font-feature-settings: 'tnum'. This is what stops your columns from jittering.
Use Instrument Serif sparingly. One or two words per surface — usually the verb in a hero headline. Never for body, never for nav, never for labels.
